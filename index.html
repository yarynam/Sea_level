Decades of High Water

It's a well-known fact that sea level is rising worldwide. However, in some particular places during last 30 years it also decreased. Decline of sea level close to the poles, ironically, is another evidence of climate change. Why? The answer to this phenomenon is geology.
By Iaryna Mykhyalyshyn

Change of the sea level worldwide

Why sea level is decreasing next to poles?

Thought the last decades Arctic ice was melting so intensively that it contributed not only to change of sea level, but the geological state of the corresponding land.

Relieved of billions of tons of glacial weight, the land has risen much as a cushion regains its shape after someone gets up from a couch. The land is ascending so fast that the rising seas — a ubiquitous byproduct of global warming — cannot keep pace. As a result, the relative sea level is falling, at a rate “among the highest ever recorded,” according to a 2007 report by a panel of experts convened by Mayor Bruce Botelho of Juneau.
